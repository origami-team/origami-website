Research

Navigational Map Reading Competencies

With the help of OriGami, we take a closer look at measuring and training navigational map reading competencies with primary and lower secondary school children.

We have developed a test instrument with which we can determine what kind of skills children have orienting and navigating with a map. We implemented the test for two selected locations and successfully evaluated it in a study. The children that participated in our study had the task of using OriGami to solve map reading tasks in a park environment. In doing so, they had to orient themselves with spatial information such as the arrangement of paths, benches, trash cans and trees, which were displayed on the map.
Our test is designed to be transferable to other locations. Those interested can download our Reproducible Paper and implement the test for the location of their choice.

We have also developed the test for virtual space to make it usable regardless of location. The results of the associated study are currently being evaluated by out team.

In the next step, we would like to use OriGami to develop learning modules that can be used to train children in their map-based orientation. For this purpose, we have equipped OriGami with a variety of map features to help children learn suitable strategies.

Collaborative learning

We are currently developing OriGami into a multi-user learning app. Our goal is to encourage children in their collaborative learning strategies and to explore children's map-based collaboration strategies. More information about the multi-user functionality will follow here soon.
